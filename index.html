MTSRL :
Multi-Task Safe Locomotion Learning for Legged Robots

SDUQuad48 Video

Abstract

Recent advances have demonstrated that reinforce- ment learning (RL) has become an effective solution for legged robot locomotion. While single-task RL has achieved remarkable success, learning a unified policy capable of handling diverse tasks for quadrupedal robots remains a significant challenge. In this paper, we propose MTSRL, a novel multi-task safe reinforcement learning framework for legged robot locomotion. To prevent overfitting to specific tasks, the framework incorpo- rates multi-task replay buffers. The multi-critic actor learning approach is adopted, where a separate critic is maintained for each task to avoid negative interference between task value estimates. Our approach enables a single policy to accomplish six blind locomotion tasks for legged robots, encompassing both quadrupedal and bipedal gaits. The quadrupedal tasks include locomotion over complex terrains, as well as control of body pitch, roll, and base height. The bipedal tasks involve locomotion over challenging terrains in both stand and handstand postures. Extensive indoor and outdoor experiments conducted on a real quadrupedal robot validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, demonstrating strong and robust multi-task locomotion capabilities.

Method

Overview of the learning framework. We first construct a multi-task reinforcement learning simulation environment, where agents corresponding to different tasks are assigned reward functions specifically designed for their respective task objectives, along with task-specific training configurations. The current proprioception $\boldsymbol{o}_t$, student historical proprioception $\boldsymbol{o}_{t-H:t}^{s}$ and full state $\boldsymbol{s}_t$ are normalized and then fed into the teacher and student policies. Both policies are trained using the constrained reinforcement learning algorithm NP3O with multi-task replay buffers, under an asymmetric actor-critic framework. The teacher and student encoders are optimized jointly using policy gradients and supervised learning, enabling mutual approximation from both directions. All teacher and student agents share a common cost critic and policy network, while each task-specific agent is equipped with an individual reward critic. The actuator network is trained offline to facilitate sim-to-real transfer by improving low-level control robustness.

Six-Skill Policy

MTSRL enables legged robots to safely and robustly perform six distinct tasks using a single policy, including quadrupedal locomotion, bipedal locomotion in stand and handstand postures over complex terrain, and body height, pitch, and roll control on flat ground. The policy relies solely on proprioception and demonstrates strong generalization and safety across diverse and challenging real-world scenarios.

Conclusion

We propose MTSRL, a unified and safe multi-task reinforcement learning framework for quadrupedal robots that enables a single policy to support both quadrupedal and bipedal gaits. To prevent overfitting to specific tasks, the framework incorporates multi-task replay buffers, while concurrent training of teacher-student policies is introduced to improve overall policy learning. The proposed method is validated through real-world experiments, where the robot successfully completes six diverse locomotion tasks relying solely on proprioceptive sensing, highlighting the strong robustness and generalization capabilities of MTSRL.
